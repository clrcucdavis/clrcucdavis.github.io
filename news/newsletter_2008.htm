Graduate Education Opportunities Series
for Chicana/o, Latina/o, and Indigenous/Native undergraduates who are
thinking about, planning, and/or preparing to pursue graduate education

Part I

Fall Quarter 2008*

November 7/11:30-1pm

Law School Panel, with Professor Jennifer Chacón, and law students, Gabriela Flowers and Erick Gutierrez, Andrews Conference Room, 2203 SSH (in the History Dept. corridor, second floor)

Nov. 7/1-2:30pm:

MALCS campus chapter Plática with Professor Jennifer Chacón,an informal conversation for Chicana, Latina, and Indigenous women of the Americas to hear about Prof. Chacón’s professional experiences with Q & A opportunities, Mayhew Conference Room, 2234 SSH (in the Sociology Dept. corridor, second floor)

 

Nov. 19/12-1:30

Men’s Platica with Professor Luís Guarnizo, Human and Community Development, an informal conversation for Chicano,Latino, and Indigenous men to hear about Prof. Guarnizo’s professional experiences with Q & A opportunities, Mee Room of the MU.

Download Flyer

Nov. 21/2-4pm

Chicana/o, Latina/o Graduate Student Dinner, University Club, to support community building, motivation, and empowerment for undergraduates, sponsored by Graduate Studies


*There will be a Winter Quarter Series and a Spring Quarter Series. If you are interested in participating or helping in any way, please let the planning committee know. Planning committee members include: Inés Hernández-Avila, ighernandez@ucdavis.edu, Alma Martinez, almartinez@ucdavis.edu, Lisceth Cruz, lizcruz@ucdavis.edu, Emilio Camacho, ecamacho@ucdavis.edu, Rosalba Valdovinos, rvaldovinos@ucdavis.edu.


The Graduate Education Opportunities series is sponsored by: the Chicana/Latina Research Center, Chicana/o Studies, La Raza Culture Days, GAAP/SRRC, and the MALCS campus chapter
