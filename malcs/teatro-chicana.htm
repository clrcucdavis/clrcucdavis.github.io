Teatro Chicana: "A Collective Memoir of the Struggle for Social Justice"

February 11, 6:30-8:30pm
Young Hall 194

The 1970s and 1980s were times of social awareness and political activism in Mexican-American communities. In San Diego, a group of Chicanas started to participate in a political theatre group whose plays addressed social, gender, and political issues of the working class and the Chicano Movement. In Teatro Chicana: A collective memoir and selected plays, seventeen women, who were a part of the theatre group Teatro de las Chicanas, come together to share their stories of why they joined the theatre and how it transformed their lives.


“These carnalas empowered themselves and thousands during the tumultuous years of the Movimiento by performing plays for working-class communities. From college campuses to the fields where campesinos toiled, estas mujeres had the courage to fight gender inequality. We need their courage today. And we need their stories for a new generation of Chicanas and for working women everywhere." Rudolfo Anaya, author of Bless Me, Ultima and Curse of the ChupaCabra


“This collection of testimonials of early Xicanistas and their work in teatro is an important contribution to the preservation of the spirit and energy that made the Chicano Movement”   Ana Castillo, author of The Guardians and So Far from God     


The purpose of this event is to promote and present the contents of this book Teatro Chicana as text material for classroom use and well as provide a personal insight of the women's struggle within the Chicano Movement. 


The MALCS campus chapter is bringing four women who contributed to the book, Felicita Nuñez, Laura Garcia, Guadalupe Beltran and Yolanda Flores. They will read selected materials, speak on teatro as an organizing tool and perform skits/actos. Following , there will be a Question and Answer session and book signing.


“Our purpose is to promote the book Tearto Chicana for classroom use which can be adopted into Chicana/o Studies, Women Studies, History, Psychology,Sociology, English Writing Classes, Political and Theater Departments.” Felicita Nuñez, co-author and co-founder of the Teatro Chicana
